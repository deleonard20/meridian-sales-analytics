# Recommendations

## 1. Basket Size (AOV) — Recover ~$17K per quarter

**Key finding:** AOV fell 10% from November's $1,661 peak to $1,494 in December, leaving an estimated $17.4K in Q4 revenue unrealized.

- Introduce a volume-discount threshold just above the current AOV (~$1,700+) to nudge basket size upward. *Tool: pricing tier model.*
- Pair hero products (e.g. Côte de Blaye) with adjacent-category items in pre-set bundles at order time. *Tool: bundle recommendation rules.*
- **Expected impact:** recover AOV to at least the $1,661 peak next high-demand quarter, closing the ~$17K quarterly gap.

## 2. Delivery Reliability (Shipper SLA)

**Key finding:** United Package ships 39% of all volume but posts the highest late rate (5.08%), just above the 5% strong-performance benchmark. Federal Shipping outperforms at 3.61%.

- Rebalance a portion of United Package's peak-season volume to Federal Shipping. *Tool: courier allocation model.*
- Formal quarterly SLA scorecards tied to the 95%+ on-time benchmark. *Tool: courier scorecard.*
- **Expected impact:** bring the blended late rate from 4.57% toward Federal Shipping's 3.61% benchmark as volume scales.

## 3. Product Revenue Concentration

**Key finding:** the top 3 products contribute 52% of top-10 revenue; Côte de Blaye alone equals more than the bottom 5 products combined.

- Track a "rising star" watchlist of products just outside the top 10 for targeted account outreach. *Tool: quarterly product performance tracker.*
- Attach top-3 hero products to bundles that pull adjacent-category items along. *Tool: bundle recommendation rules.*
- **Expected impact:** reduce top-3 share of top-10 revenue from 52% toward ~40% within 2 quarters, without slowing hero product growth.

## 4. Sales Team Concentration

**Key finding:** 3 of 9 reps (Margaret Peacock, Janet Leverling, Nancy Davolio) drive 49% of all orders; the lowest big-order conversion rate on the team is 41.79%.

- Pair top-3 reps with lower-volume reps to transfer account-handling practices that work. *Tool: structured mentorship program.*
- Review account distribution to ensure growth accounts aren't systematically concentrated with only 3 reps. *Tool: territory/account review.*
- **Expected impact:** lift the team's lowest big-order rates toward the ~48% team median, reducing single-rep concentration risk.

## 5. New Account Growth

**Key finding:** loyal accounts generate 93.79% of revenue; the 15 new accounts added in FY2025 order at less than half the basket size of loyal accounts ($811 vs. $1,647).

- Launch a structured first 2–3 order ramp plan targeting basket-size growth toward the loyal-customer benchmark. *Tool: 30-60-90 style ramp plan.*
- Validate why only 15 new accounts were added this year relative to the 74-account active base. *Tool: acquisition funnel tracking.*
- **Expected impact:** grow new-account AOV toward $1,200+ within 2 quarters of onboarding, and increase new-logo pace beyond today's 74 loyal accounts.
